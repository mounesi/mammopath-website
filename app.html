Purpose and Focus

Mammopath was created to support people affected by breast cancer—both patients and healthcare providers. We understand that breast cancer treatment is not one-size-fits-all. Each person’s experience is unique, and treatments can affect individuals differently. Our goal is to help everyone involved by offering tools to better track symptoms, understand side effects, and guide treatment decisions with clarity. Mammopath is about creating a more informed, personalized, and caring experience.

Who Is It For?

Mammopath is designed for two main groups of people. First, it’s for those who want to reduce their risk of developing breast cancer. We provide guidance, resources, and tools to help them stay proactive. Second, it’s for individuals who are currently going through breast cancer treatment. For them, Mammopath offers support to log symptoms, track medications, manage emotions, and communicate more clearly with their doctors and caregivers.

How It Works

Mammopath has two sides: one for patients and one for healthcare providers. As a patient, you can easily log how you’re feeling each day, keep track of medications or treatments, and get helpful suggestions along the way. If you’re a doctor or nurse, Mammopath helps you see a bigger picture of how your patients are doing over time. This makes it easier to catch problems early and adjust care as needed—making treatment safer and more effective.

Smart Technology That Cares

Behind the scenes, Mammopath uses artificial intelligence (AI) to make things even easier. For example, it can recognize patterns in your symptoms, give early warnings if things seem to be getting worse, and even help you write messages to your care team. This smart technology doesn’t replace your doctor—it works alongside them to make sure you get the best care, based on your unique needs.

Why Mammopath Matters

Going through breast cancer is tough. There's fear, confusion, and a lot of uncertainty. Mammopath was created to ease some of that burden. Whether you’re a patient trying to keep track of how you're feeling or a healthcare provider trying to make better decisions, this platform is here to support you. It brings clarity to complexity and reminds everyone involved: you are not alone, and you don’t have to do this without help.
